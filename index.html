Well Rehabilitation

The CCTV (Closed Circuit Tele Vision) inspection of a well provides information on its static water level, location and condition of the casing and the screen, possible changes in well diameter, and about open-hole sections. A visual impression of the formations exposed in the open-hole section help in the interpretation of the lithology, aquifer type, and the geophysical logging data.

In addition, water entry through faulty casing or a casing break above the static water level may become apparent. Especially in older wells that need to be rehabilitated, the use of the video camera is essential for checking the casing and screen conditions. Finally, the video inspection gives information on the actual depth of the well, which often differs from the original depth due to the accumulation of sand or debris.

In well rehabilitation we do:

  1. CCTV inspection
  2. geophysical logging
  3. hydrochemical - biological analyses
  4. the actual rehabilitiation
  5. anti-clogging strategies

Geology and Hydrogeology Services

The evaluation of the site-specific geology is based on geological mapping, the understanding of the structural setting of the site, and the study of rock samples with respect to their hydraulic properties. Similarly, the evaluation of hydrogeological system is based on a site-specific monitoring program and the subsequent multidimensional, numerical modeling of flow and transport. These models may be hydraulic, hydrochemical, or thermodynamic; coupling of models is possible.

Geology:

  • Regional geology
  • basin analysis
  • mapping (aerial photos and satellite imagery; outcrop maps; facies maps; structure contour maps, isopach maps)
